# Survey Question Bank

A categorized bank of engagement survey questions organized by driver. All questions
use a 5-point Likert scale unless otherwise noted:
**1 - Strongly Disagree | 2 - Disagree | 3 - Neutral | 4 - Agree | 5 - Strongly Agree**

Select at least two questions per driver to ensure reliability. For a 30-40 question
annual survey, use all drivers. For a 5-10 question pulse, rotate through 2-3 drivers
per cycle while keeping eNPS as a constant.

---

## Overall Engagement Anchor

Use one of these as the opening question on every survey.

1. I would recommend [Company] as a great place to work.
2. I am proud to work at [Company].
3. Overall, I am satisfied with my experience working here.

---

## Meaningful Work

Questions measuring whether employees find their work purposeful and significant.

1. My work gives me a sense of personal accomplishment.
2. The work I do makes a meaningful contribution to [Company]'s mission.
3. I understand how my role connects to the overall goals of the company.
4. I am doing the kind of work I find genuinely interesting.
5. My daily work feels aligned with something that matters.

---

## Manager Relationship

The highest-leverage driver. Use 4-5 questions from this category every survey.

1. My manager genuinely cares about my wellbeing.
2. My manager gives me clear, actionable feedback.
3. My manager recognizes me when I do good work.
4. I can go to my manager with problems and concerns.
5. My manager helps remove obstacles so I can do my best work.
6. My manager supports my career development and growth.
7. My manager sets clear expectations for my work.
8. I trust my manager to be honest with me.

---

## Psychological Safety

Questions adapted from Amy Edmondson's team psychological safety scale.

1. I can speak up on this team without fear of negative consequences.
2. When I make a mistake, I am not held against for it on this team.
3. It is easy to ask other team members for help.
4. My ideas and opinions are valued by the team.
5. I feel safe taking calculated risks on this team.
6. Differences of opinion are welcomed on this team.
7. I feel comfortable challenging the status quo here.

---

## Growth and Development

1. I have opportunities to learn and grow in my role.
2. My career development is supported at [Company].
3. I have a clear sense of how I can advance my career here.
4. I am given stretch assignments that help me develop new skills.
5. I receive the training and resources I need to do my job well.
6. [Company] invests in my professional development.

---

## Autonomy

1. I have the freedom to decide how best to do my work.
2. I am trusted to make decisions without unnecessary approval steps.
3. I have control over my schedule and how I structure my day.
4. I feel empowered to take initiative without waiting to be asked.
5. My ideas are taken seriously and acted upon.

---

## Recognition

1. I feel appreciated for the contributions I make.
2. Good work is recognized regularly at [Company].
3. Recognition here is timely - it happens close to when good work occurs.
4. Recognition feels genuine, not performative.
5. I know what "great performance" looks like in my role.
6. My manager notices and acknowledges my contributions.

---

## Clarity

1. I have a clear understanding of what is expected of me in my role.
2. I know how my work is prioritized and why.
3. Company goals and direction are communicated clearly to me.
4. I receive the information I need to do my job effectively.
5. I know what success looks like in my role.
6. When priorities change, I understand the reasoning behind the change.

---

## Connection and Belonging

1. I feel like I belong at [Company].
2. I have strong working relationships with my teammates.
3. I feel included regardless of my background or identity.
4. People on my team genuinely care about each other.
5. I have at least one close friend at work. *(Gallup Q12 item)*
6. I feel comfortable being my authentic self at work.
7. My unique perspective is valued by my team.

---

## Workload and Wellbeing

1. My workload is manageable and sustainable.
2. I am able to maintain a healthy balance between work and personal life.
3. [Company] genuinely cares about employee wellbeing - not just as a talking point.
4. I have enough time to do my work well without feeling rushed.
5. I feel comfortable taking time off when I need it.
6. I do not regularly sacrifice personal time to meet work demands.

---

## Company Confidence

Questions measuring trust in leadership and organizational direction.

1. I believe [Company]'s leadership makes good decisions for the organization.
2. I trust [Company]'s leadership to do the right thing.
3. I am confident in [Company]'s future.
4. Leadership communicates honestly - even when the news is difficult.
5. I believe [Company] lives up to its stated values.

---

## eNPS Question

Always include this as a standalone section, separate from the Likert items.

**Question:** On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend [Company] as a place
to work to a friend or colleague?

**Follow-up (open text):** What is the primary reason for your score?

---

## Open-Text Questions

Include 1-2 at the end of every survey. Keep them simple and open.

1. What is working well at [Company] that we should protect and continue?
2. What is one thing that would most improve your experience at [Company]?
3. Is there anything else you would like to share that we have not asked about?

---

## Onboarding Survey Questions (30/60/90-day)

Run at 30, 60, and 90 days after a new hire's start date.

**30-day questions:**
1. My onboarding experience has prepared me well to do my job.
2. I feel welcomed by my team and colleagues.
3. I understand what is expected of me in my first 90 days.
4. I have the tools and access I need to do my work.
5. My manager has been available and supportive during onboarding.

**60-day questions:**
1. I have a clear picture of how my role contributes to team goals.
2. I feel like a valued member of the team.
3. I understand [Company]'s culture and how things work here.
4. I am learning and growing in this role.
5. I would make the same decision to join [Company] knowing what I know now.

**90-day questions:**
1. I feel confident in my ability to perform well in this role.
2. I have built strong working relationships with key collaborators.
3. I have received helpful feedback on my work.
4. I can see a path to grow and advance at [Company].
5. I plan to still be at [Company] one year from now.

---

## Exit Survey Questions

Run when an employee submits resignation. Keep anonymous via a link sent to personal email.

1. What was the primary reason for your decision to leave? *(multi-select)*
   - Better compensation elsewhere
   - Limited growth or career advancement
   - Poor relationship with my manager
   - Burnout or unsustainable workload
   - Opportunity to do more interesting work
   - Relocation or personal reasons
   - Lack of belonging or inclusion
   - Company direction or leadership concerns
   - Other (open text)

2. How long had you been considering leaving before you accepted another offer?
   *(Under 1 month / 1-3 months / 3-6 months / More than 6 months)*

3. Was there anything [Company] could have done to change your decision to leave?

4. How would you rate your overall experience working at [Company]? *(1-10)*

5. Would you consider returning to [Company] in the future? *(Yes / No / Maybe)*

6. Is there anything you would like leadership to know that you felt unable to say
   while employed here?

---

## Selection Guide

| Survey type | Drivers to include | Recommended question count |
|---|---|---|
| Annual | All drivers + eNPS + 2 open text | 35-45 |
| Quarterly pulse | eNPS + 2-3 rotating drivers | 8-12 |
| Monthly pulse | eNPS + 1-2 rotating drivers | 5-7 |
| Post-reorg pulse | Clarity + Company Confidence + Connection | 8-10 |
| Manager effectiveness | Manager Relationship + Clarity + Recognition | 10-12 |
| Psychological safety audit | Psychological Safety (all 7) + Connection | 8-10 |
