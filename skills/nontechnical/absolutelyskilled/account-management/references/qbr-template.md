# QBR Template - Deck Structure and Preparation Guide

## What a QBR is (and is not)

A QBR is a strategic business review - not a product update, support call, or
upsell meeting disguised as a review. Its purpose is to confirm the customer
is achieving their goals, align on what comes next, and strengthen the executive
relationship. Done well, a QBR creates renewal momentum and opens expansion
conversations naturally.

A QBR is NOT:
- A feature demo ("here's what we shipped last quarter")
- A support status meeting ("let's go through open tickets")
- An ambush ("by the way, we'd love to talk about upgrading you")

---

## Pre-QBR Preparation (5-7 business days before)

### Data to pull
- [ ] Product usage report: logins, DAU/MAU, feature adoption, seat utilization
- [ ] ROI/value delivered: calculate against agreed success metrics
- [ ] Support history: open tickets, escalations, CSAT scores
- [ ] Health score trend for the quarter
- [ ] NPS score (if recent survey exists)
- [ ] Expansion usage patterns or limit-hitting events

### Stakeholder preparation
- [ ] Confirm executive sponsor will attend - reschedule if they cannot
- [ ] Identify any stakeholder changes (new champion, new economic buyer)
- [ ] Brief your own executive if they are attending
- [ ] Research customer's recent business news (earnings, press releases, hirings)

### Content preparation
- [ ] Write 3 strategic questions tailored to their industry and goals
- [ ] Prepare a "mutual action plan" draft to review at end of meeting
- [ ] If health score is yellow/red: prepare the candid conversation
- [ ] If health score is green: prepare one expansion opportunity to introduce

### Logistics
- [ ] Send agenda to the customer 5 business days in advance
- [ ] Confirm attendees from both sides
- [ ] Book 60-90 minutes (never shorter; allow buffer for strategic discussion)
- [ ] Prepare a collaborative doc (not just slides) for live note-taking

## QBR Deck Structure

### Slide 1 - Cover

```
[Company Logo] + [Your Company Logo]
Quarterly Business Review
[Customer Name] + [Your Company Name]
[Quarter & Year] | [Date] | Confidential
```

Keep it clean. No marketing copy on the cover slide.

### Slide 2 - Agenda

```
Today's Agenda
1. Your business update (10 min)
2. Value delivered: Q[X] results (15 min)
3. Challenges and open items (10 min)
4. Roadmap and what's ahead (10 min)
5. Mutual action plan (10 min)
6. Q&A
```

Share this with the customer in advance. Ask if anything is missing from their
perspective before the meeting starts.

### Slide 3 - Their Business Update (customer-led)

This slide is intentionally sparse - it is a prompt for them to talk, not for
you to present.

```
[Customer Company Name] - Q[X] Update

Discussion questions:
- What were your top 3 priorities this quarter?
- What changed in your business since we last met?
- What are the biggest challenges you're facing heading into Q[X+1]?
```

**Facilitation tip:** Stop talking after you read the first question. Wait.
The best intelligence you will gather in any QBR comes from this 10 minutes.
Take notes verbatim.

### Slide 4 - Success Metrics Scorecard

Show agreed success metrics side-by-side with actual results. Never hide
underperformance - address it directly with a cause and a plan.

```
| Success Metric          | Target    | Actual    | Status |
|-------------------------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| [Metric 1]              | [Target]  | [Actual]  | Green  |
| [Metric 2]              | [Target]  | [Actual]  | Yellow |
| [Metric 3]              | [Target]  | [Actual]  | Green  |
```

Color code: Green (at or above target), Yellow (within 15% of target),
Red (more than 15% below target).

Include a one-sentence narrative for each yellow or red metric:
"[Metric] missed target because [cause]. Our plan is [action] by [date]."

### Slide 5 - Value Delivered / ROI Summary

Quantify what the customer got for their investment. Use their language and
their metrics, not yours.

```
VALUE DELIVERED - Q[X]

Time saved:        [X hours/week] across [N] users = $[Y] annualized
[KPI improved]:    [X%] increase vs. pre-implementation baseline
[Cost avoided]:    $[Z] by replacing [legacy process/tool]
Adoption:          [X]% of seats active weekly, up from [Y]% last quarter

"We reduced our [process] time from [X days] to [Y days]."
  - [Champion Name], [Title]  (use a real quote if you have one)
```

> If you cannot put a dollar figure on ROI, use operational metrics: time saved,
> error rate reduction, team hours freed. Quantified value is what justifies
> renewal pricing.

### Slide 6 - Product Usage Highlights

Show usage data that demonstrates adoption and stickiness. Keep this factual
and brief - it is supporting evidence for the ROI slide, not the main event.

```
USAGE HIGHLIGHTS - Q[X]

Active users:     [N] of [N contracted] ([X]% adoption)
Sessions/user:    [X]/week (up [Y]% QoQ)
Top features:     [Feature A], [Feature B], [Feature C]
Power users:      [N] users in top 10% of activity
```

If usage is low: address it on the Challenges slide. Do not hide it here and
hope no one notices.

### Slide 7 - Challenges and Open Items

This is the most important trust-building slide in the deck. Raise issues
yourself before the customer does.

```
OPEN ITEMS AND CHALLENGES

[Issue 1]
  Status: [In progress / Resolved / On roadmap]
  Owner: [Your Name]
  ETA: [Date]

[Issue 2]
  Status: [...]
  Owner: [...]
  ETA: [...]

Your feedback: What else is not working as expected?
```

**Facilitation tip:** For each open item, say: "Here is what we know, here is
what we are doing, and here is when you will see the result." Then ask if there
is anything else they have been meaning to raise.

### Slide 8 - Roadmap Alignment

Connect your upcoming product developments to the customer's stated priorities
from Slide 3. Never dump a full product roadmap - curate only what is relevant
to them.

```
WHAT'S AHEAD - Q[X+1] ROADMAP

Relevant to your priorities:

[Feature/Release A] - [Quarter]
  How it helps you: [1 sentence tied to their goal]

[Feature/Release B] - [Quarter]
  How it helps you: [1 sentence tied to their goal]

What are you most excited about?
What do you wish we were prioritizing instead?
```

### Slide 9 - Mutual Action Plan

Close every QBR with explicit commitments from both sides. "Let's follow up"
is not an action plan.

```
MUTUAL ACTION PLAN

YOUR COMMITMENTS (what you will do)
| Action                    | Owner          | Due Date |
|---------------------------|----------------|----------|
| [Action 1]                | [Your Name]    | [Date]   |
| [Action 2]                | [Your Name]    | [Date]   |

THEIR COMMITMENTS (what they will do)
| Action                    | Owner          | Due Date |
|---------------------------|----------------|----------|
| [Action 1]                | [Their Name]   | [Date]   |
| [Action 2]                | [Their Name]   | [Date]   |

NEXT CHECK-IN: [Date] | [Format: call / in-person / async]
```

Send a follow-up email within 24 hours with the action plan summary.

### Slide 10 - Optional: Expansion Opportunity

Only include this slide if health score is green and success metrics are on
track. Framing matters - this is a "what's possible" conversation, not a sales
pitch.

```
WHAT'S POSSIBLE IN Q[X+1]

Based on your goals for [stated priority], we see an opportunity to extend
[product/capability] to [new team/use case].

Potential impact: [quantified estimate]
What it would look like: [1-2 sentences, no pricing yet]

Is this worth a 30-minute conversation with [stakeholder]?
```

> Never present pricing or a proposal during the QBR itself. The QBR is for
> strategic alignment. If interest is confirmed, schedule a separate commercial
> conversation for the following week.

## Facilitation Tips

**Start by giving them the floor.** After the agenda slide, say: "Before we
dive in, what's top of mind for you today?" Their answer will shape the entire
meeting.

**Use silence.** After asking a question, count to 5 before speaking. The
discomfort you feel is the customer formulating a real answer.

**Take notes visibly.** Write down what they say in real time. It signals that
you are listening and creates a record for follow-up.

**Watch for the real agenda.** If an attendee mentions something in passing
("actually, we've been rethinking the whole workflow..."), that is the real
conversation. Stop and explore it.

**Close with clarity.** Never end without confirming: who is doing what, by
when, and when you meet again. Ambiguity after a QBR destroys trust faster
than any product problem.

## Post-QBR Follow-Up (within 24 hours)

Send a follow-up email with:

```
Subject: QBR Follow-Up - [Customer Name] - [Date]

Hi [Name],

Thank you for the time today. Here is a summary of what we discussed and
the commitments from both sides.

KEY TAKEAWAYS
- [Top 2-3 themes from their business update]
- [Any risks surfaced and how you plan to address them]

MUTUAL ACTION PLAN
[Copy the action plan table from Slide 9]

Our next check-in is scheduled for [Date].

Please let me know if I missed anything or if priorities have changed.

[Your Name]
```

Update the account plan and CRM within 48 hours of the QBR. If health score
changed (up or down), update it immediately.
