## Overview

Clinical biostatistics covers trial design, sample size calculation, hypothesis testing, survival analysis, and statistical reporting for clinical research.

## Workflow

1. Define the study design: randomized controlled trial, cohort study, case-control, crossover.
2. Define endpoints: primary (efficacy), secondary (safety, quality of life), exploratory.
3. Calculate sample size: specify effect size, power (80-90%), significance level (0.05), and expected dropout rate.
4. Pre-specify the analysis plan: test statistics, covariates, subgroup analyses, multiplicity adjustments.
5. Analyze data: t-tests for continuous endpoints, chi-square for categorical, survival analysis for time-to-event.
6. Report effect sizes with confidence intervals, not just p-values.
7. Follow reporting guidelines: CONSORT (RCTs), STROBE (observational), PRISMA (reviews).

## Quality Gate

Clinical biostatistics is complete when the analysis plan is pre-registered, sample size is justified, and results are reported with effect sizes and uncertainty intervals.
