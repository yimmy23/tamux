# Academic Literature Review Task Guideline

Literature review work should make the search strategy, inclusion criteria, and evidence limits visible.

## Workflow

1. Define the research question, domain, population or system, intervention or topic, comparator if relevant, outcomes, and date range.
2. Use `paper-lookup` for structured academic database search and `research-lookup` for broader current research lookup.
3. Use `literature-review` when the user needs a comprehensive review, systematic review, or meta-analysis scaffold.
4. Use `bgpt-paper-search` when full-text methods, sample sizes, quality signals, or experimental details matter.
5. Track search terms, databases, inclusion/exclusion criteria, screened papers, and reasons for exclusion.
6. Synthesize by themes, methods, evidence quality, disagreement, and research gaps rather than listing papers.

## Quality Gate

Do not claim a review is comprehensive without documenting databases searched, search terms, date range, and selection criteria.
