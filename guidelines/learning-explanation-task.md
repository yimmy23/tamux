## Overview

Effective explanations adapt to the learner's current knowledge level and build understanding incrementally.

## Workflow

1. Assess the learner's current understanding and the target level of comprehension needed.
2. Start with a concrete example or analogy before the abstract definition.
3. Introduce terminology only after the concept is established.
4. Build in layers: simple model first, then refine with nuance and edge cases.
5. Check understanding with targeted questions, not just "does that make sense?"
6. Provide references for deeper exploration.

## Quality Gate

The explanation is complete when the learner can explain the concept back in their own words and apply it to a new example.
